Dear Editor,

I am pleased to submit the manuscript "Informational Task Entropy and
the Selection of Occupations Under AI-Driven Substitution" for
consideration as a research article in Entropy.

The manuscript proposes a thermodynamic-informational reframing of the
empirical phenomenon of AI-driven occupational displacement. Building
on the Maximum Entropy Production Principle (Kleidon 2010; Martyushev
and Seleznev 2006) and Jeremy England's dissipative-adaptation
programme, I distinguish two senses of occupational entropy --
thermodynamic (metabolic dissipation rate) and informational (Shannon
entropy of the task profile) -- and test the corresponding labor-market
predictions on the full US BLS 2019-2024 occupational panel (N = 707).

The principal finding is that occupations of high informational task
entropy are differentially preserved under AI-driven substitution
(Pearson r = +0.328, p = 5.8e-19 for the composite Informational
Resilience Index; p < 1e-13 for the underlying H_info coefficient once
broad AI exposure is conditioned out). The thermodynamic form, by
contrast, is dominated by non-AI confounders (COVID-era contraction,
offshoring, demographic ageing) and is not supported on the same data.
The result survives multi-source robustness checks using the Felten,
Raj, and Seamans (2021) AIOE proxy and 2-digit NAICS industry fixed
effects (the IRI coefficient retains t = +7.86, p = 1.7e-14, and adds
Delta R^2 = +0.077 on top of industry effects).

The companion dataset (Informational Resilience Index scores for
N = 698 detailed US occupations, with computer-readable CSV and full
construction code) is openly published under CC-BY 4.0 at Zenodo,
alongside this manuscript's preprint. The paper sits at the
intersection of non-equilibrium thermodynamics, information theory,
and the economics of automation, and would be accessible to Entropy's
interdisciplinary readership.

The manuscript has not been submitted elsewhere and is not under
consideration at any other journal. The author has no conflicts of
interest to declare and no funding to acknowledge (independent
researcher).

I look forward to your editorial response.
